Project Management Vocabulary

Introduction to Project Management

Project management is the process of planning, executing, and closing projects. Effective project management involves balancing scope, time, and cost, while ensuring quality and stakeholder satisfaction. Below are key vocabulary terms that are essential for understanding project management.

Core Vocabulary

Phases of Project Management

  • Initiation: The process of defining a new project or a new phase of an existing project.
  • Planning: The phase where project goals are established and the roadmap to achieve them is created.
  • Execution: The phase where the project plan is put into action, and deliverables are created.
  • Monitoring and Controlling: The process of tracking project performance and making adjustments as necessary.
  • Closure: The formal completion of a project, including the final deliverables and project documentation.

Key Roles in Project Management

  • Project Manager: The individual responsible for leading the project and ensuring it meets its goals.
  • Stakeholder: Anyone who has an interest in the project, including team members, sponsors, and customers.
  • Business Analyst: A professional who helps define project requirements and analyze business needs.
  • Scrum Master: A facilitator for an Agile development team, ensuring adherence to Agile principles.

Agile Methodology

  • Sprint: A time-boxed period during which specific project tasks are completed and reviewed.
  • Backlog: A prioritized list of work for the team that is derived from the project roadmap and its requirements.
  • Retrospective: A meeting held at the end of a sprint to reflect on what went well and what could be improved.
